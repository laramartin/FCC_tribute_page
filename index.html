Katamari

The game series that allowed us to clean the world




The videogame series was created by Keita Takahashi and developed and published by Namco.

Katamari, å¡Š, means "mass" in Japanese and it's the name of the ball the player moves around rolling up things. The user begins rolling up small things. As the items are getting stuck on the ball, it becomes larger, making possible to roll up bigger things. You go from getting paperclips and pins to cars and houses. And even stars!

At the end of the stage, the King of All Cosmos evaluates the size of the ball and gives a rating.










The first game was released on 2004 for PlayStation 2. Since then, a total of 12 titles have been published, including games for mobile. On the following table you can see the titles that have been released and the platforms the games were made for.
